Shi-ang Qi

I am a 3rd-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Computing Science at the University of Alberta, where I am advised by Dr. Russell Greiner. I also work closely with Dr. Neeraj Kumar for machine learning research in healthcare.

Previously, I obtained my M.Sc. degree in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Alberta in 2020, where my supervisor was Dr. Jie Chen. And I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Biomedical Engineering from Huazhong University of Science and Technology in China.

My research interests involve machine/deep Learning for healthcare, bioinformatics, survival analysis, and causal inference for medicine tasks. Currently, I'm particularly interested in building interpretable survival models (individual survival distributions) for identifying actionable factors of breast cancer, which is collaborating with the Alberta's Tomorrow Project.
